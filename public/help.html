🎯 The goal

An attacker has breached the organization. The Incident Master (GM) secretly holds a four-card attack chain — one card from each phase: Initial Compromise → Pivot & Escalate → C2 & Exfil → Persistence. The Defenders use Procedure cards and roll a d20 to detect those cards.

  • Defenders win when all 4 attack cards are detected.
  • Attackers win if the team runs out of turns first (default 10 turns).

Everyone on the same Wi-Fi opens the table in a browser. The GM's hidden cards are never sent to players or viewers — they only appear when the GM reveals them, so there is nothing to peek at.

👥 Roles

🎴 Game Master

Runs the incident. Sees the hidden attack chain, builds the scenario, adjudicates detections, plays Injects & Consultants, and controls the rules. Open /gm · needs the PIN · up to 2 GMs.

🛡️ Defender

The blue team. Picks a Procedure each turn and rolls the d20 to find the attacker. Chats on the team channel. Open / · 1–5 players, one shared team.

👁️ Viewer

Spectates the whole board live (great for a projector or stream). Has a separate viewers-only chat. Open / → choose Viewer · unlimited.

Chat visibility

Defenders and the GM share the team channel; Viewers and the GM share the viewers channel. Viewers never see team chat and vice-versa — the GM sees both and can post to either.

🛠️ Setting up a game (Game Master)

  1. Open /gm and enter the PIN shown in the server console. You'll land on the board with the GM panel.
  2. Pick a deck (Core v3.1 or the Expansion with Consultants).
  3. Build the scenario — either hit 🎲 Randomize whole scenario, or hand-pick: one card for each of the four attack phases, plus 4 Established procedures (the team's strong suits).
  4. (Optional) Fill the Detection map: for each attack card, choose which procedures would detect it (read the card art). This lets the game auto-reveal on a successful, relevant roll instead of you revealing manually.
  5. (Optional) Tweak the rules: turn limit, success threshold, Established bonus, inject nudge, and cooldown.
  6. Share the network URL with players/viewers, then press ▶ Start incident. needs ≥1 player, or Force start

🎮 How a turn works

  1. Defender picks a Procedure from the board and clicks 🎲 Roll d20. Any card can be enlarged by clicking it.
  2. The roll resolves: d20 + Established bonus (if the procedure is Established) + modifiers. If the total meets the success threshold, it succeeds. The die animates and the math is spelled out.
  3. Detection: on a success the GM reveals the attack card that procedure uncovered. With a detection map the matching card auto-reveals (or the GM chooses if several match); without one the GM judges and reveals manually.
  4. Cooldown: a procedure that was just rolled is locked for a few turns (shows a ❄ badge) so the team has to vary its approach.
  5. Injects & Consultants: the GM can play an Inject (a complication) — and a natural 1 or 20 auto-plays one. An inject can carry a standing roll modifier (e.g. “−3 to all procedures”) that then applies to every roll. Consultants are one-time boosts the GM hands the team.
  6. Repeat until all 4 cards are detected (win) or the turn limit is reached (loss).

Tips for Defenders

  • Lean on your Established procedures — they add the bonus to your roll.
  • Talk it out in team chat; the GM can drop hints there.
  • Watch the ⏳ turns-left counter — it turns red when you're nearly out of time.

Every value above is GM-editable in the panel. A negative inject modifier subtracts from the total — Established bonus included.

🤖 Solo mode (vs. an AI Incident Master)

No group? Open /solo, enter a name and an optional scenario flavor (e.g. “a regional hospital”), choose a narrator model, and Begin. A local LLM plays the Incident Master:

  • It writes a unique opening scene with realistic IOCs (attacker IP, C2 domain, file hash, compromised account, host, CVE, ATT&CK techniques).
  • Each success detects the next stage and narrates what you found, hinting at the next step; each failure gives a believable real-world reason it stalled.
  • Every incident is freshly randomized, so no two games are alike. It still plays fully even if the LLM is offline (templated narration).
